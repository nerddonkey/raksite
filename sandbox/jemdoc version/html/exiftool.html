Renaming and Organizating Media

Here we rename and organise such that we:

  1. Can change nondescript files names to ones that reflect the date-time information related to when the capture was made, e.g., “1303218998 Tue 19 Apr 2011 2316+1000.MTS”.

  2. Move the files into folders (creating them if necessary), in the same example case, “2011 04 (Apr)”.

Depending on the camera we can rely on different tags to identify the time the capture was actually taken:

For SONY DSC and SONY Video Cam

exiftool -ext MTS "-FileName<DateTimeOriginal" -d "%a %d %b %Y %H%M%z%%-c.%%e" .
exiftool -ext JPG "-FileName<DateTimeOriginal" -d "%a %d %b %Y %H%M%z%%-c.%%e" .
exiftool -ext MTS -ext JPG "-FileName<DateTimeOriginal" -d "%a %d %b %Y %H%M%z%%-c.%%e" .

Fixing Invalid Creation Dates

Here we assume we want to set all the creation dates of JPG files in the directory to their respective modification dates:

#!/bin/bash
FILES=*.JPG
for f in $FILES
do
    echo "$f"
    SetFile -d "$(GetFileInfo -m "$f")" "$f"
done
